What to Expect / Instructions

Writing problems

Use "Write a problem" above!

Note that the database rolls over from year to year. Because OTIS has such a large community of students, problems submitted to OTIS should no longer be considered secure. If you think you might want to submit a problem to a different competition later in life where problem security is at all relevant, you should not submit it here.

You are asked to select a difficulty tier for your problems. This tier the amount of time that testsolvers get to spend on it. You can't change difficulty after submission!

  • 🔥 × 1: AIME 1-3, solvers get 10 minutes
  • 🔥 × 2: AIME 4-6, solvers get 15 minutes
  • 🔥 × 3: AIME 7-9, solvers get 20 minutes
  • 🔥 × 4: AIME 10-12, solvers get 25 minutes
  • 🔥 × 5: AIME 13-15, solvers get 30 minutes

Solving problems

You can play in two modes, ranked mode and casual mode.

  • In ranked mode, you're committing to trying the problems seriously, meaning that we'll use the statistics from ranked mode to evaluate difficulty. We use ranked data when writing acknowledgments for testsolvers in the published pacukeut. When you try problems, we'll record the number of attempts and time taken. Please try your best on every problem you open.
  • In casual mode, you can browse and try whatever problems you like. There's an answer checker, but it doesn't store anything and there's no timers or locks.

You can switch from ranked to casual at any time. If you try to switch from casual to ranked, we'll allow it too, but any problems that were published before you switched are no longer eligible for you to try in ranked mode.

After you solve or give up on a proposal, you can read spoilers and comment on the thread for the problem. You can also upvote the problem using the heart icon to indicate that you like it.

How Evan picks the 15 problems

For now, final selection of problems and placements is done by Evan.

The selection of problems are based on the following criteria.

  • Popularity: as evidenced by comments, upvotes, and Evan's own personal taste.
  • Solution write-up: if the solution needs a lot of editing to be presentable (or worse, is just a sketch with no details), then the problem is less likely to be chosen.
  • Solve rate: problems with too few correct testsolves are less likely to be chosen. We want problems that OTIS students can actually get right.

Note that problem proposals submitted earlier gain an advantage because there is more time for people to upvote it and more opportunities for it to be testsolved. Thus, submitting problems well before the deadline is advised.
