What to Expect

Writing new problems

  • Use "Write a new problem" once you're in the Probase collection.
  • Please make sure to provide a full solution and triple-check your answer for accuracy to avoid frustration for the testsolvers.
  • Please use a descriptive title that doesn't spoil the problem but gives an idea for what the problem is about.
  • Note that the database rolls over from year to year. If you ever want to withdraw a problem from the database, you need to manually ask OTIS staff to have it withdrawn. Otherwise you should assume by default it can be seen by future years of OTIS as well and possibly appear in subsequent years' mock AIME's.
    • In practice, we probably won't go out of our way to dig out ancient problems for use, but the point is that right now we don't systematically erase old problems.
    • Even then, because OTIS has such a large community of students, problems submitted to OTIS should no longer be considered secure, even if you ask for the problem to be withdrawn later. If you think you might want to submit a problem to a different competition later in life where problem security is at all relevant, you should not submit it here.

Solving problems

By default you're in ranked mode. Problem statements stay hidden until you choose to fight a proposal, where you enter a timed session and have five chances to answer it correctly. The time limit is based on the difficulty tier, see below. This data is used as legit testsolving data for statistics, etc.

If you'd rather not race the clock, you can switch to casual mode. There you can browse and try any problem at your own pace and upvote the ones you like, with nothing recorded. Each solution stays hidden until you reveal it, so you won't be spoiled before you're ready. You can switch back to ranked mode later, but any problem that already exists by then is no longer eligible for a timed solve (it stays browsable casually instead).

If you already know a particular problem — say you helped write it — you can reveal its solution from the problem page without doing a timed session. That forfeits your chance to fight that one problem for a recorded time, but lets you read and discuss it.

Difficulty tiers

There are five tiers of difficulty, and testsolvers get an amount of time per problem when testsolving it based on the tier:

  • 🔥 × 1: AIME 1-3, solvers get 10 minutes
  • 🔥 × 2: AIME 4-6, solvers get 15 minutes
  • 🔥 × 3: AIME 7-9, solvers get 20 minutes
  • 🔥 × 4: AIME 10-12, solvers get 25 minutes
  • 🔥 × 5: AIME 13-15, solvers get 30 minutes

After you solve or give up on a proposal, you can read spoilers and comment on the thread for the problem. You can also upvote the problem using the heart icon to indicate that you like it.

Selection process for the 15 slots

For now, final selection of problems and placements is done by Evan.

The selection of problems are based on the following criteria.

  • Popularity: as evidenced by comments, upvotes, and Evan's own personal taste.
  • Solution write-up: if the solution needs a lot of editing to be presentable (or worse, is just a sketch with no details), then the problem is less likely to be chosen.
  • Solve rate: problems with too few correct testsolves are less likely to be chosen. We want problems that OTIS students can actually get right.

Note that problem proposals submitted earlier gain an advantage because there is more time for people to upvote it and more opportunities for it to be testsolved. Thus, submitting problems well before the deadline is advised.
